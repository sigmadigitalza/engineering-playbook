Change,
Explained.

Animation isn't decoration — it's how an interface explains change over time. Done well it orients, confirms and connects; done badly it distracts, or makes people ill. Toggle each to feel the difference between motion that works and motion that just moves.

01

Easing

Nothing in the real world starts or stops abruptly. Natural acceleration is what separates a living interface from a mechanical one.

02

Timing

Duration sets the mood. Too fast and a change goes unnoticed; too slow and the interface feels like it's wading through treacle.

~300ms · natural
03

Choreography

Related elements shouldn't all arrive at once. A small stagger turns a jarring pop into a guided, readable sequence.

All at once
04

Continuity

When one thing becomes another, show the journey. Growing a card into its detail view keeps the user oriented — no teleporting.

Card
Collapsed card
05

Feedback

Motion confirms that an action landed. A press that depresses and a result that springs in tell the user the system heard them.

Submit✓
Idle
06

Reduced Motion

Large sliding, scaling motion can trigger nausea and migraines. Honour the user's setting by replacing movement with a gentle fade.
